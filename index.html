Detailed Metrics Guide (Affan's Notes)

1. R (Risk) & Position Sizing

R is the maximum money you risk per trade. Position size is calculated based on R and SL distance.

Formula: Quantity = R / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)

Example: R=500, Entry=500, SL=480. SL Dist=20. Qty = 500/20 = 25 Qty.

2. RR & ARR (Risk Reward Ratio)

RR is for single trades, ARR is the average for your whole system. If ARR > 2, you are a champion.

RR = (Target - Entry) / (Entry - SL) | ARR = Avg Profit (R) / Avg Loss (R)

3. Expectancy (Accuracy)

It defines if your system is profitable or not. Should always be > 0.5R for a good system.

Expectancy = (Win Rate * Avg R Gain) - (Loss Rate * Avg R Loss)

4. Intensity Metric

The total power of your trading system at the end of the quarter.

Intensity = Expectancy * No. of Trades * R

5. TOR (Total Open Risk)

Total risk currently exposed in the market. Use 1R-3R in sideways market, max 4R-5R in bullish market.

TOR = Sum of (Entry - Current SL) * Qty / Risk R
